⚠ Laser Safety

Last updated: April 2026

Lasers are dangerous. They can cause permanent eye damage, skin burns, and fires. You are solely responsible for the safe operation of your laser equipment.

General Safety Rules

NEVER leave a laser running unattended. Fires can start in seconds, especially when cutting wood, paper, acrylic, or fabric.

Always wear laser safety eyewear rated for your laser's wavelength (typically 445nm for diode lasers, 10600nm for CO2 lasers).

Ensure adequate ventilation or use a fume extraction system. Cutting and engraving materials produces smoke and fumes that can be harmful.

Keep a fire extinguisher (CO2 or dry chemical) within arm's reach of your laser at all times.

Never look directly at the laser beam, even with safety glasses on.

Dangerous Materials

Do NOT cut or engrave the following materials without proper research, ventilation, and safety equipment:

PVC / Vinyl — produces hydrochloric acid gas (extremely toxic and corrosive)

Polycarbonate (Lexan) — absorbs infrared radiation, melts and burns instead of cutting cleanly

ABS — produces hydrogen cyanide gas

HDPE / Milk jugs — melts, catches fire easily

Polystyrene / Styrofoam — catches fire and produces toxic fumes

Fiberglass — produces toxic dust

Coated carbon fiber — produces toxic fumes

Safe Materials (with ventilation)

Wood, plywood, MDF, cork, paper, cardboard, leather (not chrome-tanned), felt, cotton, acrylic (cast), glass (engraving only), anodized aluminum (marking only), stainless steel (marking only with appropriate laser).
